Tendering Process

Kimangu Day Secondary School is committed to a fair, transparent, and competitive procurement process. We invite qualified suppliers, contractors, and service providers to participate in our tendering opportunities. Below is our standard tendering process:

Steps to Apply for Tenders

  1. Tender Advertisement: All our tenders are advertised on this website, our social media and our notice board.
  2. Purchase of Tender Documents: Interested bidders can purchase tender documents by paying the prescribed fee.
  3. Tender Preparation: Bidders prepare their bids according to the specifications and requirements outlined in the tender documents.
  4. Bid Submission: Completed bids should be submitted in sealed envelopes by the specified deadline.
  5. Tender Opening: All bids are opened publicly on the specified date and time.
  6. Evaluation: Bids are evaluated based on technical specifications, pricing, supplier qualifications, and other criteria.
  7. Award: The tender is awarded to the most qualified bidder who meets all requirements.
  8. Contract Signing: A formal contract is signed with the successful bidder.

Required Documents

  • Business Registration Certificate
  • Tax Compliance Certificate
  • PIN Certificate
  • VAT Registration Certificate (if applicable)
  • Proof of Financial Capacity
  • Proof of Relevant Experience and References
  • Qualification of Key Personnel
  • Company Profile

Important Note:

  • All tender bids must be addressed to the Chairman, Tender Committee.
  • Each tender bid must be submitted in a separate envelope, with the tender number clearly indicated on the envelope.
  • Ensure that all relevant documents are securely enclosed in the envelope.

Terms of Engagement

General Terms & Conditions

Contract Terms

All contracts will be governed by Kenyan law. Contract duration varies by tender type and will be specified in the tender documents.

Payment Terms

Standard payment terms are 30 days after delivery and acceptance of goods/services. No advance payments unless specified in tender documents.

Quality Standards

All goods and services must meet the standards specified in the tender documents. The school reserves the right to reject any substandard deliveries.

Delivery Timeframes

Strict adherence to agreed delivery schedules is required. Late deliveries may attract penalties and/or termination of contract as specified in the contract.

Confidentiality

All information provided during the tendering process is confidential and should not be disclosed to third parties.

Eligibility & Disqualification

Who Can Apply

  • Registered businesses with valid business permits
  • Tax compliant businesses
  • Businesses with relevant experience in the field
  • Businesses with adequate financial capacity
  • Businesses with qualified personnel

Grounds for Disqualification

  • Late submission of bids
  • Incomplete documentation
  • False or misleading information
  • Collusion or unethical practices
  • Failure to meet mandatory requirements
  • Conflict of interest
  • Prior poor performance

Procurement Ethics & Anti-Corruption Policy

Kimangu Day Secondary School is committed to ethical procurement practices. We have a zero-tolerance policy for corruption, bribery, or any form of unethical conduct. Any attempt to influence the tender process through illegal means will result in immediate disqualification and may be reported to relevant authorities. All our staff involved in procurement are bound by a strict code of ethics, and we expect the same from our suppliers and service providers.
